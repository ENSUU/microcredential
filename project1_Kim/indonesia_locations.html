Borobudur Temple

Summary

The Borobudur Temple is a Buddhist temple located in Central Java. The temple dates all the way back to the 8th and 9th centuries, during the reign of the Syyaliendra Dynasty. The temple was built on three tiers, each representing the three superimposing spheres of Buddhist cosmology. These include:

  • Kamadhatu (the desire realm)
  • Rupadhatu (the form realm)
  • Arupadhatu (the formless realm)
This temple is the world's largest Buddhist monument and a priceless ancient center for pilgrimage and education in Mahanyana Buddhism. If you would like to visit the Borobudur Temple, learn more about available tours here!
